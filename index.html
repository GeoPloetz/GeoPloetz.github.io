Geospatial Geoarchaeology | Maya Lowlands | Digital Heritage

Reconstructing ancient landscapes through lidar, drones, geoarchaeology, and visualization.

I study how ancient communities engineered, inhabited, and transformed tropical landscapes over time. My research integrates lidar, drone mapping, GIS, 3D modeling, excavation, coring, soils, paleoenvironmental reconstruction, and digital heritage to understand long-term human–environment interaction in the Maya lowlands.

About

Chris J. Ploetz is a Ph.D. candidate in Geography and the Environment at The University of Texas at Austin. His work sits at the intersection of geography, archaeology, environmental science, remote sensing, and digital heritage. His dissertation and broader research program focus on integrated geospatial, geoarchaeological, and paleoenvironmental approaches to ancient Maya landscapes.

Across field, lab, and classroom settings, Chris is especially committed to giving students hands-on experience with practical methods—GIS, lidar, drone mapping, excavation, 3D modeling, field documentation, and environmental analysis—that they can carry into graduate research, public service, cultural resource management, geospatial careers, environmental work, and community-based heritage projects.
